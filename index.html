10 Interesting things about India

India, which is also called Bharat, is a country in South Asia. It's the seventh largest country in the world by area, bounded by Himalayan moutains on the north, Indian ocean on the south,Bay of Bengal on the south-east and Arabian sea on its south-west. It also shares land borders with 6 other countries.

Let's see some of its fun facts:

  • Chess
  • Khumb Mela
  • Prominent American Indians
  • Languages in India
  • Indian road and rail networks
  • Taj Mahal
  • Dance forms in India
  • Indian seasons
  • Tea in India
  • Golden Temple

  1. India is the birthplace of chess. The original word for "chess" is the Sanskrit chaturanga, meaning "four members of an army"-which were mostly likely elephants, horses, chariots, and foot soldiers.

  2. The Kumbh Mela (or Grand Pitcher Festival) is a huge Hindu religious festival that takes place in India every 12 years. In 2001, 60 million people attended, breaking the record for the world's biggest gathering. The mass of people was photographed from space by a satellite.

  3. Indians hold prominent places both internationally and also in the united states. For example, Satya Nadella CEO of Microsoft,Shantanu Narayen CEO of Adobe systems, Indra Nooyi chairman and CEO of pepsi co, Sundar pichai CEO of Google inc, Ajay Banga precident and CEO of Mastercard.

  4. Hindi and English are the official languages of India. The government also recognizes 17 other languages (Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Nepali, Manipuri, Konkani, Kannada, Kashmiri, Malayalam, Marathi, Oriya, Punjabi, Sanskrit, Sindhi, Tamil, Telugu, and Urdu). Apart from these languages, about 1,652 dialects are spoken in the country.

  5. India has the world's third largest road network at 3,400,233 miles. It also has the world's Fourth largest rail network, comprising 119,630 kilometres (74,330 mi) of total track and 92,081 km (57,216 mi) of running track over a route of 66,687 km (41,437 mi) with 7,216 stations.

  6. The Taj Mahal ("crown palace") , a beautiful tourist attraction in india was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan (1592-1666) for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal (1593-1631). This architectural beauty has been called "marbled embroidery" for its intricate workmanship. It took 22,000 workmen 22 years to complete it.

  7. Dancing is one of India's most highly developed arts and was an integral part of worship in the inner shrines of every temple. It is notable for its expressive hand movements. There are eight classical dance forms in india, Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Kuchipudi, Odissi, Kathakali, Sattriya, Manipuri and Mohiniyattam.

  8. India experiences six seasons: summer, autumn, winter, spring, summer monsoon, and winter monsoon.

  9. India is the world's largest tea producer, and tea (chai) is its most popular beverage. India produces 70% of the world's spices.

  10. The Golden Temple in India feeds a vegetarian meal to over 100,000 people a day regardless of race, religion and class.
